About Me

My name is Braden Zaremba, I am a sophomore at Penn State University where I am pursuing a bachelors degree in Cyber Security Operations and Analytics with a course focus in Application Development. In addition to pursuing my degree I am also involved with the Competitive Cyber Security Club (CCS0) on campus.

While I am firmly anchored in Cyber Security major I have a strong passion for coding that bridges the gaps between my professional, academic, and personal life.The blend of my backround in Cyber Security and my passion for coding has complemented each other in a way that has given me a much deeper understanding of both fields, such as identifying a SSH port being left open in a script designed to automate the deployment of a company-wide computer update.

My programming experience has been primarily focused on Java however I do have some experience with Python, SQL, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. One of my goals that I have set for myself is to continue to expand my knowledge of programming languages and to build a large GitHub page full of my personal passion projects. My ambition for coding has led me to want to pursue a career in the software development to blend my passions for coding and cyber security.

Aside from my passion for coding my roots are still strongly set in Cyber Security. I have a strong understanding of the information security fields principles, tools, and concepts. Some of the tools that I have used in my coursework include Wireshark, Nmap, Metasploit, Spartan, Snort, and Kali Linux. My current goal in the field of Cyber Security is to obtain my CompTIA Security+ certification showing my ambition to better myself and show my commitment to the field.

When I step away from my academics and personal projects I like to immerse myself in playing video games, such as my current favorite Baldur's Gate 3. If I am not playing a video game them I am usually at one of the Penn State's Hockey or Football games. In addition to my allegiance to my school's sports teams I am also a big Baltimore Ravens. While it may be a bit of a drive I try to make it a point to see at least one game with my girlfriend every year.

In the future I hope to leverage my skills in both Cyber Security and coding to work in the software development industry. Where it is my dream to work as a software engineer. Proving to myself that I can take my hobbies and turn them into a profession where others may benefit from my work. I am excited to see where my journey takes me and I am looking forward to the challenges that I will face along the way.
